Priority fee is the gas fee that you send along with your transaction in order to facilitate the network usage. It‚Äôs size ranks your transaction from high priority (faster) to low priority (slower). Slippage is the percentage change you are willing to accept if the price changes between the time you submit your transaction and the time it is confirmed. For example: you decide to buy a coin at $1.00, you submit your transaction, with slippage set to 20%, but by the time your transaction is confirmed the price of the coin has gone up 30% and is now worth $1.30. The transaction failed because your slippage was too low. High slippage is for high volatility, it also has higher risk because your transactions might fail with too low slippage, but with too high slippage you might get a wildly different price than you were hoping. This applies both ways, to buys and sells.

Finally, bribe is exactly what it says it is. It is an extra fee that you can send along with the transaction to convince validators to let your transaction jump the line. This is how MEV bots work, they scan transactions in the queue, and when they see one with lower priority they send theirs with a big bribe in order to jump in front, knowing that the slower priority transaction will come through after theirs, then they immediately sell to make profit on the margins.
